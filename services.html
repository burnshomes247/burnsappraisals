Appraisal Services

Common Reasons for an Appraisal

Every appraisal starts with a thorough on-site inspection and a careful analysis of recent comparable sales.

Pre-Listing / Home Sale

Know your home's value before you list. A certified appraisal helps you price confidently, negotiate from a position of strength, and avoid leaving money on the table. Trusted by homeowners and real estate agents alike.

Pre-Purchase / Buyer Appraisal

Make sure you're not overpaying. An independent appraisal before you close gives you an objective value — separate from your lender's appraisal — so you can negotiate with confidence.

Mortgage Refinancing

Lenders require an appraisal before approving a refinance. Alicia's reports are accepted by all major lenders and delivered fast so your transaction stays on schedule.

Estate & Probate

When settling an estate, an accurate date-of-death valuation is essential for equitable distribution and IRS reporting. Alicia has extensive experience with trust, estate, and probate appraisals accepted by attorneys and courts.

Divorce & Legal Proceedings

Marital asset division requires a defensible, independent opinion of value. Alicia provides credible appraisal reports that hold up in mediation and court.

Property Tax Appeal

If your assessed value is too high, an appraisal can be the key to a successful appeal. Alicia provides the documentation you need to make your case to the county.

PMI Removal

Once your equity reaches 20%, you may be able to eliminate private mortgage insurance. An appraisal documenting your current value is often the fastest path to removing PMI and lowering your monthly payment.

Bankruptcy

Courts and trustees require accurate property values during bankruptcy proceedings. Alicia provides certified appraisals accepted by federal bankruptcy courts across the DC metro area.
